Funeral Services Interactive Activity

Challenge

How might we build an accessible, engaging, and meaningful online interaction that allows learners to practice arranging funeral processionals and recessionals?

Process

My team at Florida State College at Jacksonville was tasked with designing an online funeral directing course. Previous funeral directing courses failed to provide learners an opportunity to try an important practical skill they'd need in the field: arranging a funeral processional and recessional. For this design, I worked with an instructional designer (ID), web developer, and another multimedia designer, with a funeral director as our subject matter expert (SME).

  1. Our challenge started with an image.

    In the previous iteration of this course, learners only had this image with text to teach them how to arrange a Roman Catholic processional. Through consultations to empathize with the SME and define the problem, we discovered an underlying issue: the course was not providing hands-on experience to fully prepare learners to be new funeral directors.

  2. In the ideation stage, we determined how best to provide an accessible, engaging learning experience. Learners needed to be able to try out what they believed was correct prior to seeing the results for self-assessment. We also concluded it should be low-stakes (not graded) and repeatable, so that they could continue to work on what they knew if they ever felt uncertain.

  3. Our prototyping led to a fully accessible drag-and-drop learning activity so that learners could play with the elements (necessary people and items) on the “board” (the interior of a religion building) to test their knowledge of funeral processionals and recessionals.
