Prototype #2

Challenge

How might we shift the current course design process to view and include subject matter experts as co-designers alongisde LX designers?

For this second prototype, upon receiving user feedback from multiple users, I adjusted the wicked problem to further fit my intended goal. Most of the changes to my design took place toward the beginning of the experience, as that is where most users had concerns. My aim is still to “design for designers” as Mitchel Resnick says.

  1. Begin initial meeting with informal discussion between subject matter expert and LX designers based on “5 Whys” analysis. Together, the team will explore SME’s motivation, goals, and expectations of the design process. Questions will be provided to LX designers to focus discussion toward getting SME to think about how they’d wish to improve the course, what has worked/not worked in the past, and discovering a challenge they want to solve. Not every question provided must be asked. This would also be an opportunity to address any unperceived or misperceived needs of the SME.

    If time, scheduling, or other constraints prohibit “5 Whys” discussion, an optional online questionnaire will be made available prior to the initial meeting. These questions would be the same as in-person discussion, but without the ability have a natural conversation around them as would happen in an in-person discussion.

    Tool used:

    Meeting: Microsoft Teams

    Optional Online Questionnaire : Google Forms

  2. End initial meeting by starting brainstorming collaboration session. The brainstorming method used can be tailored based on SMEs preference. The purpose of the session will be centered around further uncovering the goals/problem the SME hopes to achieve/solve with this design process. We would collaborate using Office 365 or Miro (depending on the SME’s comfort level with technology). The SME and LX Designers will continue to brainstorm on their joint document after the meeting ends.

    Tools used:

    Meeting: Microsoft Teams

    Collaboration: Microsoft Word 365

    Collaboration: Miro

  3. Prior to Kick-off meeting, SME and LX Designers would work collaboratively on the brainstorming document to converge on their top goal & the enduring knowledge they want to impart.

    LX Designers will encourage SMEs to include their department colleagues to provide feedback on what the course’s aim should be. SMEs will only need to provide name and contact info of colleagues to LX designers and they will facilitate the feedback session.

    Tool used:

    Collaboration: Microsoft Word 365

    Collaboration: Miro

  4. During Kick-off meeting, goal/problem is established, along with learning outcomes and objectives.

  5. Course development then begins. This will also be the prototyping phase. LX Designers will build prototypes and SMEs will provide feedback through on-going meetings.

    LX Designers will encourage SMEs to include their past and/or present learners to provide feedback on these prototypes, if they so desire. SMEs will only need to provide name and contact info of learners to LX designers and they will facilitate the feedback session.

    Tools used:

    Meeting: Microsoft Teams

  6. Finally, the course will be piloted by the SME for one semester prior to being useable by all faculty. Right now, communication at this phase in our development cycle ceases between designers and SME. Assuming intrinsic motivation is improved through changes in this process, that open line of communication would strengthen. Ideally, SME would continue to provide designers with feedback throughout their pilot and continue a collaborative brainstorming document on ways to improve the next iteration of the course.

    Tool used:

    Collaboration: Microsoft Word 365

    Collaboration: Miro
